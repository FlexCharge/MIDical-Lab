Step 7 — Reintroduction rule (only applies to a cooled MID)

If the MID is currently in cooldown, re-run steps 1–4 against the most recent 7 days of post-rest activity. <50% DEGRADING → reintroduce at 50/50 split; ≥50% still DEGRADING → pull again, extend rest by 15 days, retry. Not applicable to the sample CAID — no pull/cooldown has occurred in the window.

Step 8 — Control comparison (interpretive, not a separate trigger)

Re-run steps 1–4 against the CONTROL arm of the cohort — the 50% of in-scope CIT 1st-attempt traffic routed through the merchant's default cascade. Three readings:

  • BOTH arms degrade together → cause is upstream of the experiment (merchant-level or issuer-portfolio-wide). Pull may still fire on the experiment side, but the report should note the upstream cause.
  • ONLY the experiment side degrades → the new-MID hypothesis is directly supported. This is the strongest evidence the experiment can produce.
  • ONLY the control side degrades → unusual; investigate the cascade.
